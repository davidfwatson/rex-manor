# Writing Style: Avoid AI Slop (tropes.fyi)

When drafting any prose for this site that David will publish — blog posts, news
updates, listserv replies, neighborhood emails — scan for and remove the AI
writing tropes catalogued at [tropes.fyi](https://tropes.fyi) before delivering.

**Why:** These posts go out under David's name to neighbors. AI-slop patterns
(em-dash addiction, "It's not X, it's Y", "serves as" dodges, bold-first
bullets, "the X? A Y." rhetorical questions) make a post read like ChatGPT
output even when the substance is fine, and readers notice.

**How to apply:** Before delivering a drafted post, do a quick pass against the
worst offenders below. One instance of any pattern is fine; clusters or repeated
use are the problem.

## Worst offenders to scan for

**Sentence structure:**
- "It's not X — it's Y" / "Not X. Not Y. Just Z." (the single biggest AI tell)
- "The result? Devastating." (self-posed rhetorical question + one-word answer)
- Anaphora abuse ("They have built X, but not Y. They have built A, but not B.")
- Tricolon stacking (rule-of-three back-to-back-to-back)
- False ranges ("from innovation to cultural transformation" — no spectrum exists)

**Word choice:**
- Magic adverbs: quietly, deeply, fundamentally, remarkably, arguably
- AI vocabulary: delve, leverage (verb), utilize, robust, streamline, harness, certainly
- Grandiose nouns: tapestry, landscape, paradigm, synergy, ecosystem, framework
- "Serves as" / "stands as" / "marks" / "represents" instead of plain "is"
- "It's worth noting" / "Importantly" / "Interestingly" / "Notably" as filler

**Tone:**
- "Here's the thing" / "Here's the kicker" / "Here's where it gets interesting"
- "Think of it as..." / "It's like a..." (patronizing analogy)
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "The truth is simple" / "History is clear" (asserting obviousness instead of proving it)
- "Let's break this down" / "Let's unpack" / "Let's dive in"
- Vague attributions ("experts say", "observers note") without naming anyone
- Invented compound labels ("the supervision paradox", "the acceleration trap")

**Formatting:**
- Em-dash addiction (a human uses 2-3 per piece; AI uses 20+)
- Bold-first bullets where every list item starts with a bolded phrase
- Unicode decoration: arrows, "smart quotes", any character you can't type easily

**Composition:**
- Fractal summaries (every section summarized, then the doc summarized)
- Dead metaphor (beating one metaphor into the ground)
- Historical-analogy stacking ("Apple didn't X. Facebook didn't Y. Stripe didn't Z.")
- One-point dilution (restating the same thesis 8 ways across 4,000 words)
- Signposted conclusions ("In conclusion...", "To sum up...")
- "Despite its challenges..." (acknowledge-then-dismiss formula)
- Short punchy fragments as standalone paragraphs ("He published this. Openly. In a book.")

## Pre-delivery checklist

1. How many em-dashes? If more than a few in a short post, kill most.
2. Any "It's not X, it's Y" sentences? Rewrite.
3. Any bullet list where every bullet starts with bold? Unbold or restructure.
4. Any "serves as / stands as / represents" where "is" would do? Swap.
5. Any tricolon stacks or anaphora runs? Break the pattern.
6. Any vague "experts" or "observers"? Name them or cut.
7. Any "In conclusion" / "To sum up"? Delete; the reader can tell.
