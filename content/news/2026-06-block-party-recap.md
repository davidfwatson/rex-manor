Hello neighbors,

What a great afternoon! It was wonderful to see so many of you—familiar faces and new ones alike—at our annual Neighborhood Block Party on **Sunday, June 7**. San Pierre Street was full of food, music, and conversation, and it was exactly the kind of day that reminds us how lucky we are to live in Rex Manor.

![Neighbors lining up for food under the canopies on San Pierre Street](/images/block-party-2026/food-tables.jpg)

These events only happen because so many neighbors roll up their sleeves and pitch in. We want to say a big **thank you** to everyone who made it possible:

* The volunteers who passed out announcement flyers around the neighborhood.
* The food prep crew who cut the fruit, tomatoes, bell peppers, onions, cucumbers, and lettuce, and juiced the lemons for lemonade.
* The neighbors who lent their grills, canopies, and tables.
* The setup crew who came early to close off the street, haul the picnic benches over from the school, set out the food, and get the grills going.
* Everyone who brought food and desserts to share.
* Our grill masters and the folks who served the food.

* Gary from the Monta Loma neighborhood, who twisted balloons for the kids—and donated all the balloons, too!
* Everyone who stayed to clean up, returning the picnic benches to the school, breaking down tables and canopies, and gathering up the trash and leftovers.

A few **special thanks** are in order as well:

* To Wayne, Michelle, and David Watson for writing the grant that funds the block party and organizing the food purchasing.
* To Lawrence Shing for hauling all the borrowed grills, tables, and canopies with his trailer, sous-viding the drumsticks, and making the lemonade.
* To Susan Keith for graciously lending her electricity so we could have music and keep the ice cream cold in a mini freezer.
* To Mark Lakata for providing the sound system and music.

![Neighbors sharing a meal together at the block party](/images/block-party-2026/neighbors-dining.jpg)

### Giving Back

Our neighborhood's generosity really showed this year. Thanks to your donations, we collected food and **$400 in checks** for the **Community Services Agency**, the local non-profit that provides emergency assistance, food and nutrition programs, and senior and homeless services right here in our community. Thank you for caring for our neighbors near and far.

### See You Next Year

It truly takes a village, and ours came through in a big way. Thank you again to everyone who came out, lent a hand, and shared the day with us. We can't wait to do it all again—**hope to see you at next year's block party!**

If you'd like to stay in the loop on neighborhood happenings, you can always [join our mailing list](https://rexmanor.org/join/).

— David Watson
