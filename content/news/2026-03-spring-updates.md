Hello neighbors,

Spring is officially here, and it's a great time to get out and enjoy everything Mountain View has to offer. As our city continues to evolve, there are always new things to discover and exciting opportunities to connect with our community and the broader region.

Here are a few updates and events to keep an eye on as we head into April:

### Downtown Pop-Up: "Don't Eat Me"
If you find yourself strolling down Castro Street, be sure to check out the newest addition to our downtown area! Through the city's wonderful pop-up program in partnership with MOMENT, storefronts are being activated with fresh, creative energy. The active pop-up shop right now is called "Don't Eat Me," located at 293 Castro Street. It features a fantastic collective of local artists. This program is a wonderful way to support local creatives while keeping our downtown vibrant and engaging.

### "From Day One: Silicon Valley 2026" at the CHM
Our local tech industry continues to bring incredible events and brilliant minds to our doorstep. On **April 8, 2026**, the [Computer History Museum](https://computerhistory.org/) will host [**"From Day One: Silicon Valley 2026"**](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/from-day-one-silicon-valley-2026-tickets-1421971578269). Events like this are a great reminder of how fortunate we are to live in a hub of innovation. The tech community plays such a positive role in driving progress and creating a dynamic environment right here in our city.

### Neighborhood Life
As a quick reminder for those who might have missed it on the [rex-manor-neighbors Google Group](https://groups.google.com/g/rex-manor-neighbors), information regarding the **Neighborhood Grant Program** applications is now available. This is a great opportunity to secure funding for projects that enhance our community. Be sure to check the group for the forwarded details and deadlines. It’s initiatives like this that help us keep Rex Manor such a wonderful place to live.

Have a wonderful weekend, and I hope to see you around the neighborhood!

— David Watson
